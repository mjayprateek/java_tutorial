        HowTo: Python Convert a String Into Integer
       </h1>

       <span class="drop_cap">
        H
       </span>
       ow do I convert a string such as x='12345' to an integer (int) under Python programming language? How can I parse python string to integer?
       <br/>
       <span id="more-12907">
       </span>
       <br/>
       You need to use
       <kbd>
        int(s)
       </kbd>
       to convert a string or number to an integer. For testing purpose, defined a string called x='123456', run:
       <p>
       </p>
       <center>
        <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
        </script>
        <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7825705102693166" data-ad-slot="2404767461" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
        </ins>
        <script>
         (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
        </script>
       </center>
       <p>
        You may also need to use
        <kbd>
         float(s)
        </kbd>
        to convert a string or number to a floating point number or combination of both as follows:
       </p>
       <p>
        <kbd>
         <br/>
         int(s)
         <br/>
         float(s)
         <br/>
         int(float(s))
         <br/>
        </kbd>
       </p>
       <h2>
        Examples
       </h2>
       <pre class="python"> 
x = <span style="color: #483d8b;">'123456'</span>
 </pre>
       <p>
        Confirm the object type:
       </p>
       <pre class="python"> 
<span style="color: #008000;">type</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span>
 </pre>
       <p>
        Sample outputs:
       </p>
       <pre>&lt;type 'str'&gt;</pre>
       <p>
        Convert a string to an int and store it to i, run:
       </p>
       <pre class="python"> 
i = <span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #008000;">type</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>i<span style="color: black;">)</span>
 </pre>
       <p>
        Sample outputs:
       </p>
       <pre>&lt;type 'int'&gt;</pre>
       <p>
        However, the following string to int will fail:
       </p>
       <pre class="python"> 
x=<span style="color: #483d8b;">'123456.334'</span>
y=<span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span>
 </pre>
       <p>
        Sample outputs:
       </p>
       <pre>
ValueError                                Traceback (most recent call last)
/home/vivek/<ipython console=""> in <module>()
ValueError: invalid literal for int() with base 10: '123456.334'
</module></ipython></pre>
       <p>
        Try the following solution:
       </p>
       <pre class="python"> 
x=<span style="color: #483d8b;">'123456.334'</span>
y=<span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span><span style="color: #008000;">float</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span><span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #008000;">type</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #008000;">type</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>y<span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #ff7700;font-weight:bold;">print</span> y
a=<span style="color: #483d8b;">'12.5'</span>
b=<span style="color: #483d8b;">'13.5'</span>
<span style="color: #ff7700;font-weight:bold;">print</span><span style="color: black;">(</span> <span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span><span style="color: #008000;">float</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>a<span style="color: black;">)</span><span style="color: black;">)</span>+ <span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span><span style="color: #008000;">float</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>b<span style="color: black;">)</span><span style="color: black;">)</span> <span style="color: black;">)</span>
 </pre>
       <p>
        Finally, you can also use floating point class for decimal arithmetic:
       </p>
       <pre class="python"> 
x=<span style="color: #483d8b;">'123.34'</span>
<span style="color: #ff7700;font-weight:bold;">import</span> <span style="color: #dc143c;">decimal</span>
i=<span style="color: #008000;">int</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>Decimal<span style="color: black;">(</span>x<span style="color: black;">)</span><span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #ff7700;font-weight:bold;">print</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>i<span style="color: black;">)</span>
<span style="color: #008000;">type</span><span style="color: black;">(</span>i<span style="color: black;">)</span>
 </pre>
